Dr. George Gey collected bodies. As a cell biologist, he was supplied with an endless amount of cancer tissue from patients in local hospitals, no questions asked.

In 1956, Gey found the perfect specimen for his work: a tumor of cervical tissue that grew so aggressively that it practically refused to die... a lump of cancer and blood.

Gey was going to create the first immortal human cancer cell line.

The tumor was scraped into a tube with a solution of chicken blood and cow fetus and umbilical cord and salt. Slowly it turned in a metal drum, swirling and growing in the dark.

This was not the first Specimen Gey tried creating. Most other cancer cells died in the dark tubes after a few days, but this tumor was different. All it needed was an energy source: the solution.

Gey had created a piece of human body that would never die. He shared his Specimen with the medical community. It was blasted with radiation and infected with polio, AIDS, COVID. It was killed millions of times over and still, undead it remained, allowing us to unlock the limits of the human body.

The body was now a Specimen.
