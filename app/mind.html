Dr. Rufus Weaver collected bodies. As an anatomist and professor, he dissected countless cadavers, supplied to him through the state.

In 1888, Weaver found the perfect specimen: a cadaver so emaciated by consumption that it was practically free of adipose tissue... a bag of skin and bone.

Weaver was going to create the first human nervous system dissection.

Weaver cut into the cadaver and peeled back layers of corpse. Separating skin from muscle from tendon from fascia, tugging at flesh and chipping at bone to isolate the somatic nerves from the body. The brain was hollowed and stuffed and the eyes were filled with a hardening substance. Each nerve was painted white and pinned to a board.

Weaver was proud of his monumental achievement. He displayed his dissection at the 1893 Worlds Columbian Exhibition in Chicago and received national, even worldwide praise.

The body was now a Specimen.
